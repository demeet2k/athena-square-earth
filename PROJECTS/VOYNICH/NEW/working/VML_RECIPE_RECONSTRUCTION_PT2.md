### Pharmacological Analysis

**HELLEBORE** (*Helleborus niger*): Contains cardiac glycosides (helleborin, hellebrin) and steroidal saponins. Premier purgative from Hippocrates through the Renaissance. Used for "melancholia" (which included cardiac symptoms, edema, and depression). Extremely toxic without proper preparation — requires extensive detoxification of the root.

**FENNEL** (*Foeniculum vulgare*): Contains anethole and fenchone — carminative, digestive aid, anti-spasmodic. Used in every medieval pharmacopoeia as a corrective for harsh purgatives. The Antidotarium Nicolai specifically prescribes fennel as a digestive corrective alongside hellebore.

**CORIANDER** (*Coriandrum sativum*): Contains linalool — aromatic, carminative, mild analgesic. Used in compound medicines as an aromatic corrective and flavor-masker. Specifically paired with hellebore in Dioscorides' De Materia Medica as a corrective for the bitter root.

**COMBINATION LOGIC:** Hellebore purges excess humors (the active therapeutic). Fennel prevents the cramping and intestinal spasm that hellebore causes. Coriander masks the intensely bitter taste and provides additional carminative support. This is STANDARD medieval compound purgative formulation — documented in the *Circa Instans*, *Antidotarium Nicolai*, and Hildegard of Bingen's *Physica*.

**TRIA PRIMA SIGNIFICANCE:** The recipe achieves perfect tria prima balance: Salt (hellebore root body) + Sulphur (fennel oil) + Mercury (coriander volatile spirit). This is the pharmaceutical section's ONLY recipe where all three identified plants map to different tria prima categories — suggesting the tria prima framework was an intentional organizational principle for compound medicines.

### Pharmacological Analysis

**BELLADONNA** (*Atropa belladonna*): Contains atropine and scopolamine — anticholinergic alkaloids that numb tissue and reduce inflammation when applied topically. Used in medieval plasters and ointments for joint pain, neuralgia, and surgical sites. Topical application is SAFER than oral — avoids systemic toxicity while delivering local analgesic effect.

**CHAMOMILE** (*Matricaria chamomilla*): Contains α-bisabolol and chamazulene — potent anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing compounds. Used in every medieval formulary for wound care and inflammation. Acts synergistically with belladonna by reducing inflammation while belladonna provides numbing.

**CORIANDER** (*Coriandrum sativum*): Contains linalool — aromatic penetration enhancer. In modern transdermal drug delivery, terpenes like linalool are recognized as skin penetration enhancers that facilitate transport of active drugs through the stratum corneum. Medieval compounders may have observed empirically that aromatic herbs improved the effectiveness of topical medicines.

**COMBINATION = TOPICAL ANALGESIC OINTMENT:**
- Chamomile: anti-inflammatory base (reduces swelling, calms tissue)
- Coriander: aromatic carrier + penetration enhancer
