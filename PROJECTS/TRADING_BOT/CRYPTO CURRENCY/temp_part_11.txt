PART IV: MAYAN CYCLES — THE CALENDAR SYSTEMS
The Maya developed the most sophisticated calendrical systems in the pre-modern world, tracking multiple interlocking cycles simultaneously.
A. THE TZOLKIN — SACRED CALENDAR OF 260 DAYS
Structure
260 = 13 × 20 — the product of two sacred numbers:
13 = tones/numbers (representing cosmic creative forces)
20 = day-signs (archetypal energies)
Why 260?
Human gestation: ~260 days (9 months × 29 days)
Corn growing cycle in highlands: ~260 days
Interval between zenithal sun passages at ~15° latitude
Smallest number with 13, 20, 4, 5, and 52 as factors or LCM components
The 20 Day-Signs (Nawales)
The 13 Tones
How the Tzolkin Works
Day 1: 1 Imix (Tone 1, Sign 1) Day 2: 2 Ik' (Tone 2, Sign 2) ... Day 13: 13 Ben (Tone 13, Sign 13) Day 14: 1 Ix (Tone returns to 1, Sign continues to 14) ... Day 20: 7 Ajaw (Tone 7, Sign 20) Day 21: 8 Imix (Sign returns to 1, Tone continues to 8)
The two cycles (13 and 20) mesh like gears. After 260 days, both return to their starting position simultaneously.
Each of the 260 unique combinations has specific meaning:
1 Imix: Powerful beginnings, creative chaos, new inception
7 Ajaw: Mystical enlightenment, attuned mastery
13 Kimi: Transcendent transformation, ultimate letting go
Current Tzolkin Count
The Tzolkin has been counted continuously by Maya daykeepers (Ajq'ijab') for thousands of years — one of the longest unbroken calendrical traditions.
Example: January 20, 2026 in Tzolkin ≈ 7 Ajaw (check with Maya calendar converters for precise date)

B. THE HAAB' — CIVIL CALENDAR OF 365 DAYS
Structure
365 = (18 × 20) + 5
18 months of 20 days each = 360 days
1 short month (Wayeb') of 5 days = 5 days
Total = 365 days (approximate solar year)
The 18 Months + Wayeb'
Wayeb' (the 5 "unlucky" days):
Considered dangerous, liminal, unpredictable
Portal between years
Rituals performed for protection
Calendar Round — 52-Year Cycle
LCM(260, 365) = 18,980 days = 52 solar years
The Calendar Round is when both Tzolkin and Haab return to their starting configuration simultaneously.
New Fire Ceremony (Xiuhmolpilli): Every 52 years, the Aztecs performed the New Fire ceremony:
All fires extinguished throughout the empire
At midnight on the final day, priests attempted to kindle new fire on a mountain
If successful: new 52-year cycle begins; fires relit throughout the land
If failed: believed the world would end
Why 52?
52 = 4 × 13 = elements × cosmic numbers
52 weeks in a solar year
Human lifespan in ancient times ≈ 52 years

PART V: GREEK AGES OF MAN — HESIOD'S METALLIC DESCENT
Source: Works and Days (c. 700 BCE)
Hesiod presents five races of humanity, each worse than the last (with one exception), named after metals of decreasing value.
The Five Ages in Detail
THE GOLDEN AGE (Χρύσεον Γένος)
Ruler: Cronus (Saturn)
Characteristics:
Humans lived like gods, free from worry and toil
No need to work — Earth produced food spontaneously
No old age — they remained youthful until death
Death was like falling asleep
After death, became "daimones" (guardian spirits)
From Hesiod:
"They lived like gods without sorrow of heart, remote and free from toil and grief: miserable age rested not on them; but with legs and arms never failing they made merry with feasting beyond the reach of all devils."
Correspondence:
Vedic: Satya Yuga
Biblical: Garden of Eden
Mayan: Before the first Sun
THE SILVER AGE (Ἀργύρεον Γένος)
Ruler: Zeus (Jupiter)
Characteristics:
Inferior to Golden in body and mind
Spent 100 years as children under their mothers' care
Lived only a short time as adults
Spent their brief adulthood in strife with one another
Refused to worship the gods
Zeus destroyed them for impiety
After death, became "blessed spirits of the underworld" (second rank)
From Hesiod:
"A child was brought up at his mother's side a hundred years, an utter simpleton, playing childishly in his own home. But when they were full grown and reached the measure of their prime, they lived only a little time and suffered greatly through their foolishness."
Correspondence:
Vedic: Treta Yuga (though not as degraded)
Represents: Humanity needing guidance but resisting it
THE BRONZE AGE (Χάλκεον Γένος)
Ruler: Zeus
Characteristics:
Created from ash trees (symbol of hardness)
Terrible, strong, warlike
Dedicated to warfare and violence
Weapons, armor, and houses all made of bronze
No iron existed yet
Destroyed themselves through their own violence
After death, went to Hades unnamed (no honor)
From Hesiod:
"Their hearts were as hard as adamant, fearsome men; great was their strength and unconquerable arms grew from their shoulders on their stalwart limbs... bronze was their armor and bronze their houses, and of bronze were their implements."
Correspondence:
Vedic: Dvapara Yuga (early phase)
Historical: Actual Bronze Age (3300-1200 BCE)
THE HEROIC AGE (Ἡρώων Γένος)
Ruler: Zeus
Unique Features:
The only age NOT named after a metal
The only age that is BETTER than the preceding age
The age of demigods and heroes
Characteristics:
More just and noble than the Bronze Age
Demigods (half divine, half human)
The great heroes of Greek myth: Heracles, Achilles, Odysseus
Fought the great wars: Seven Against Thebes, Trojan War
After death, taken to the Isles of the Blessed (Elysium)
From Hesiod:
"These are called demigods, the race before our own... but death and fate caught some of them, and some went to the ends of the earth, to the isles of the blessed beside deep-swirling Ocean, where they live untouched by sorrow in the islands of the blessed."
Correspondence:
No direct Vedic equivalent
Represents: Heroic intervention in the descent — great souls who partially reverse the decline
Why does the Heroic Age interrupt the descent? The Heroic Age may represent:
Divine intervention through incarnation (avatars, demigods)
The possibility of heroic resistance to cosmic degradation
A "grace period" before the final fall
THE IRON AGE (Σιδήρεον Γένος)
Ruler: Zeus
Characteristics:
Hesiod's own age (and ours)
Constant labor and sorrow
Brief rest from toil day or night
Degeneration of family bonds
Children dishonor parents
Host-guest relationship violated
Might makes right
Shame and righteous indignation (Aidos and Nemesis) will flee to Olympus
Only suffering will remain
From Hesiod:
"For now truly is a race of iron, and men never rest from labor and sorrow by day, and from perishing by night; and the gods shall lay harsh trouble upon them... Strength will be right, and reverence will cease to be; and the wicked will hurt the worthy man, speaking lies against him... Aidos and Nemesis, with their sweet forms wrapped in white robes, will go from the wide-pathed earth and forsake mankind to join the company of the deathless gods."
Correspondence:
Vedic: Kali Yuga
Biblical: Post-Fall world
Mayan: Fourth/Fifth Sun degradation
