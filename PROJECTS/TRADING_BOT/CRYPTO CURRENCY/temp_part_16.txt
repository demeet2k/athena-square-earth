PART I: WHAT McKENNA GOT RIGHT
The Genuine Insights
Terence McKenna was a brilliant pattern-recognizer who intuited several profound truths. Before we critique, let's honor what he saw clearly:
Insight 1: Time is Fractal
McKenna understood that temporal patterns repeat at multiple scales simultaneously. The same structure that governs a day governs a year governs an epoch. This is absolutely correct and forms the foundation of our holographic time model.
Insight 2: The I Ching Encodes Temporal Structure
The I Ching is not merely a divination system — it is a complete state-space mapping of change itself. McKenna intuited that the 64 hexagrams represent the full set of possible temporal configurations. This is correct.
Insight 3: Novelty and Habit Oscillate
McKenna proposed that time alternates between periods of:
NOVELTY — acceleration, complexity, unpredictability, emergence
HABIT — deceleration, repetition, predictability, consolidation
This maps perfectly to our cycle phases:
Novelty = Transition/Creation phases
Habit = Stability/Maintenance phases
Insight 4: Historical Resonance
McKenna noticed that historical events seem to "rhyme" — similar patterns recurring at different scales. The fall of Rome resonates with the fall of other empires. Personal crises mirror civilizational crises. This fractal resonance is real.
Insight 5: Acceleration Toward Transition
McKenna perceived that novelty was increasing — that the rate of change itself was accelerating. This matches the Kali Yuga end-conditions and the characteristics of Fourth Turning crisis periods.

The Core Idea (Correctly Stated)
McKenna's Essential Thesis:
"The I Ching is a mathematical description of time's fractal structure. By analyzing its sequence, we can derive a wave function that describes the ebb and flow of novelty across all scales of time. This wave reveals that history is not random but patterned, and that patterns at one scale predict patterns at other scales."
This thesis is CORRECT in principle.
The errors were in execution, not conception.

PART II: WHAT McKENNA GOT WRONG
The Fatal Flaws
Flaw 1: Arbitrary Endpoint Selection (OVERFITTING)
The Error: McKenna chose December 21, 2012 as the "zero point" where the timewave would reach infinite novelty (singularity). He selected this date because:
The Mayan Long Count ended on this date
He had a visionary experience suggesting it
He then worked backwards, adjusting his wave's parameters until historical events aligned.
Why This Is Wrong: This is textbook overfitting. Given enough adjustable parameters, any wave can be made to "predict" known historical events. The test of a theory is forward prediction, which Timewave Zero failed spectacularly — nothing exceptional happened on December 21, 2012.
The Correction: Remove the fixed endpoint. Time doesn't collapse into a singularity — it transitions to a new cycle. December 21, 2012 was the END of a Great Cycle (5,125 years) and the BEGINNING of a new one, not the end of time itself.
