I.S3 — Truth typing rules (minimum required)
TypingRules must define necessary and sufficient conditions for each truth class.
I.S3.a — OK conditions
A claim is typed OK iff all of the following hold:
PinsDigest closure resolves and matches corridor requirements.
Required artifacts for OK are present and verify (witness bundle, replay capsule, expected digests).
Replay determinism holds: replay produces exactly the committed digests.
All invariants in the claim’s InvariantSet evaluate true under pinned schemas.
Budget caps are respected.
No revocation or quarantine applies.
I.S3.b — NEAR conditions
A claim is typed NEAR iff:
All pin closure and replay requirements hold,
The claim fails at least one OK equality check but satisfies bounded residual constraints under a pinned metric,
A ResidualLedger is present with:
metric digest,
bound,
measured residual value,
scope,
expiry pulse,
promotion obligations,
replay pointer reproducing the measurement,
Budget caps are respected,
Disclosure rules are satisfied.
NEAR must never be emitted without a residual ledger.
I.S3.c — AMBIG conditions
A claim is typed AMBIG iff:
The corridor cannot decide OK or NEAR due to underdetermination,
A bounded candidate set exists within compute budget,
An EvidencePlan is emitted specifying:
candidate set,
discriminators,
routes to obtain missing evidence,
promotion rules,
stop rules and budgets,
The ambiguity is explicitly represented in the outcome artifacts.
AMBIG must never be emitted without an evidence plan.
I.S3.d — FAIL conditions
A claim is typed FAIL iff any of the following holds:
required pins missing,
required artifacts missing for the attempted truth class,
replay mismatch occurs where replay is required,
budget caps exceeded and not downgradable,
invariants violated beyond admissible bounds,
domain violations (maps applied outside declared domain),
illegal edge kinds under corridor.
FAIL must include a FailureWitness if reproducible under pinned environment; otherwise it must be explicitly labeled non-reproducible only if corridor policy permits (not permitted for publish corridors).
I.S3.e — CONFLICT conditions
CONFLICT is typed when reproducible verifier disagreement exists under identical pins, or when two admissible paths yield incompatible digests and no pinned tie-break can select one.
CONFLICT must produce a ConflictPacket and fence/quarantine actions per QuarantineRules.

I.S4 — Promotion/demotion and disclosure obligations
I.S4.a — Promotion rules
PromotionRules define deterministic obligations for:
NEAR→OK: specify required refinements, new witnesses, tightened bounds, or additional replays.
AMBIG→NEAR/OK: specify discriminators and evidence acquisition routes.
Promotions are append-only (Ch20): an OK outcome supersedes a NEAR/AMBIG outcome via explicit EvidenceRow supersession, not by overwriting.
I.S4.b — Demotion rules
Demotions are not performed by retyping old evidence. Instead, demotion occurs by quarantine/revocation overlays:
if a previously OK witness is revoked or invalidated, it remains historically present but becomes non-routable by fence/quarantine.
demotion is therefore represented by new quarantine records and by updated routing outcomes at later pulses.
I.S4.c — Disclosure obligations
DisclosureRules define mandatory propagation of:
residual ledgers (NEAR),
evidence plans (AMBIG),
quarantine/fence references (FAIL/CONFLICT),
expiry and renewal constraints.
Any route transcript that cites NEAR evidence must include disclosure pointers; omission is a violation and must be typed FAIL at routing time.

J.S2 — Operators
J.S2.a — Residual computation operator
Given metric (d) pinned by MetricDigest, define:
[
r := d(\mathsf{Observed}, \mathsf{Expected})
]
computed deterministically under pinned numeric and ordering policies.
If metric produces a vector (v), compute scalar residual via AggregationRule:
MAX: (r = \max_i |v_i|)
SUM: (r = \sum_i |v_i|)
L2: (r=\sqrt{\sum_i v_i^2})
L1: (r=\sum_i |v_i|)
CUSTOM: (r = \mathsf{Agg}(v)) where (\mathsf{Agg}) is pinned.

J.S4 — Protocols
J.S4.a — Ledger issuance protocol
To issue a NEAR ledger:

Select metric by digest and resolve MetricSpecRef.

Bind Calibration record; compute calibration digests.

Construct PromotionObligations (NEARPromotionChecklist).
