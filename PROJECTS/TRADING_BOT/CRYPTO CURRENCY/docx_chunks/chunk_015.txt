❀ Flower — Geometric meaning: seed as coordinate-free object, state as charted representative, navigation as path selection

The aether seed (\mathcal{A}) may be regarded as a coordinate-free object: a point in a moduli space of typed relations modulo gauge. In this interpretation:

where the projection is “evaluate back to seed.” The fiber over (\mathcal{A}) contains all representations consistent with (\mathcal{A}) under pinned legality. Snap selects a canonical section of this fibration (or a best-approximate section, recorded as NEAR).

☁ Cloud — Uncertainty: partial seeds, NEAR instantiation, and AMBIG navigation plans
14.C1 Partial seeds and evidence plans
If the input seed is missing required typing, gauge constraints, boundary policies, or legality pins, canonicalization cannot produce a unique (\mathcal{A}). In this case, compilation must return (\mathrm{AMBIG}) with:
a bounded candidate set of completions ({\mathcal{A}_1,\dots,\mathcal{A}_m}) (when enumerable within (\chi)), and
an evidence plan specifying discriminators that collapse ambiguity (additional typing facts, unit constraints, corridor selections, or canonical gauge selections).
The compiler is forbidden to guess missing constraints while claiming OK.

14.C2 NEAR instantiation and residual disclosure
If the seed is canonical but exact corridor intersection is not achievable (Snap does not reach zero defect within budgets), compilation may emit (\mathrm{NEAR}) state with residual ledger bundle (\mathsf{RL}). NEAR is admissible only if:
all residuals are computed under pinned metrics,
bounds are explicit,
scope is explicit (which parts of state/navigation are affected), and
replay capsules reproduce the residual computation.
NEAR states must be treated as first-class: navigation that depends on them must propagate residual disclosures and may be forbidden under strict corridors (e.g., publication).

14.C3 Probabilistic seeds and pinned inference rules
If the seed includes stochastic components (random variables, distributions, empirical measures), the seed is still an invariant object only if its probabilistic semantics are pinned:
distribution families and parameters,
sampling policies (if any),
estimator definitions (if approximate),
and replay policies (seeds/RNG discipline).
Absent pinned inference rules, probabilistic compilation cannot be import-verifiable and must be typed AMBIG/FAIL under strict corridors.

14.C4 Navigation under uncertainty is routable, not suppressed
Uncertainty in compilation and navigation is represented by routable truth typing:
(\mathrm{AMBIG}) produces evidence-plan routes rather than semantic routes,
(\mathrm{NEAR}) produces residual-ledger routes with explicit disclosures,
(\mathrm{FAIL}) produces fences and conflict packets,
(\mathrm{OK}) produces publishable routes.
This turns “compiler uncertainty” into explicit graph content.
