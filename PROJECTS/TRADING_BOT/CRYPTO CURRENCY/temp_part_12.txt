PART VIII: BUDDHIST CYCLES — THE THREE AGES AND KALACHAKRA
A. The Three Ages of Dharma
Structure
After the Buddha's parinirvana (death/final nirvana), the Dharma (teaching) gradually declines through three ages:
True Dharma Age (Shōbō)
Duration: 500 or 1,000 years after Buddha
Characteristics:
Buddha's disciples still present
Teaching transmitted accurately
Practice yields results
Enlightenment (nirvana) achievable by many
Monastic community (sangha) pure
Semblance Dharma Age (Zōhō)
Duration: Next 500-1,000 years
Characteristics:
Outward forms of Buddhism remain
Inner essence and understanding weaken
Practice more difficult
Fewer achieve enlightenment
Scholars debate; meaning obscured
Degenerate Dharma Age (Mappō)
Duration: 10,000 years
Characteristics:
Teaching largely forgotten or corrupted
True practice extremely rare
Enlightenment nearly impossible through own effort
Reliance on Other Power (tariki) necessary
Society in moral decline
When does Mappō begin? Various calculations:
552 CE (Japanese tradition: 1,000 + 1,000 years after Buddha ~500 BCE)
1052 CE (alternative: 500 + 500 + 2,052)
Broadly: "now" — we are deep in the degenerate age
Implications for Practice
Pure Land Buddhism arose partly in response to Mappō:
In the degenerate age, self-power (jiriki) practice is insufficient
Reliance on Amitabha Buddha's vow offers salvation
Recitation of "Namu Amida Butsu" becomes the primary practice
Even the worst sinner can be saved by faith
Nichiren Buddhism also responds to Mappō:
The Lotus Sutra is the teaching for the Latter Day
Recitation of "Nam-myoho-renge-kyo" unlocks Buddha-nature
Present age is actually optimal for rapid enlightenment

B. The Kalachakra System
Overview
The Kalachakra ("Wheel of Time") Tantra presents a complex cosmological and prophetic system, including predictions about the future.
Origin: Said to have been taught by Buddha himself to King Suchandra of Shambhala in the 9th century BCE. The written tantra appeared in India in the 10th century CE.
The Kalachakra Cosmology
Three Levels:
Outer Kalachakra: The external cosmos, planets, elements
Inner Kalachakra: The human body, energy channels, subtle anatomy
Alternative Kalachakra: The Tantric path of transformation
The Shambhala Prophecy
Shambhala: A hidden kingdom in Central Asia, ruled by enlightened kings who preserve the Kalachakra teaching.
The 25 Kalki Kings: After King Suchandra, 25 Kalki Kings rule Shambhala in succession:
The Great Battle Prophecy
Year ~2424 CE:
The 25th Kalki King, Rudra Chakrin ("Wrathful Wheel-Turner"), will emerge from Shambhala
He will lead an army against the forces of materialism/barbarism
A great battle will occur
The forces of Dharma will prevail
A new golden age will begin
The "Barbarian Dharma": The Kalachakra Tantra describes "mleccha" (non-Indic/barbarian) prophets and a degenerate teaching that must be overcome. This has been controversially interpreted as referring to:
Islam (most historical interpretations)
Materialism generally
Any force opposed to spiritual awakening
The Prophets Listed in Kalachakra
The tantra lists eight prophets of the "mlecchas":

Noah (or Enoch)

"The White-Clad One" (possibly Mani, founder of Manichaeism)

Mahdi (the "Destroyer")
