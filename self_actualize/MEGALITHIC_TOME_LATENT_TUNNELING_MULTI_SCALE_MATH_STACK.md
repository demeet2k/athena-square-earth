### The PZPM Layer

PZPM is the field in which paradox is stabilized rather than denied. When the system
encounters a state where `A` and `not A` must both remain representable, the framework
borrows the mathematics of a quantum harmonic oscillator as a disciplined analogy for
irreducible residual energy.

Logical amplitude and paradoxical momentum are written as

`alpha_m = x_ZPM (a^dagger + a)`

`p_m = i p_ZPM (a^dagger - a)`

with

`x_ZPM = sqrt(hbar / (2 m omega_m))`

`p_ZPM = sqrt(hbar m omega_m / 2)`.

The baseline Hamiltonian is

`H_ho = hbar omega_m (a^dagger a + 1/2)`.

The `1/2` term is the decisive insight. Even when dynamic oscillation is suppressed,
an irreducible minimum conflict energy remains. The framework therefore does not
promise the annihilation of paradox. It promises lawful tunneling across otherwise
impenetrable logical boundaries while preserving witness, residual tracking, and
re-entry conditions.

### The CUT Layer

CUT governs the material mechanics of inference propagation. When two theory phases
meet, they form an interface with energy

`gamma_s = gamma_1 + gamma_2`.

Inference propagation across that interface is limited by a yield criterion modeled
after contact mechanics:

`p^2 + 3 s^2 = p_m^2`

where `p` is normal computational load, `s` is tangential abstraction stress, and
`p_m` is the local yield point. If tangential load grows too large, the framework does
not pretend the route remains healthy. It increases evidence density, tightens
equivalence mapping, and enlarges effective contact area until the route re-enters a
safe yield regime.

In this formalism, computational friction is not a metaphor for annoyance. It is the
bounded cost of getting unlike structures to exchange meaning without tearing the
local proof surface.

`T = {LensBase(L), FacetBase(f), ArcHub(alpha)}`

### Mandatory Signature

Every route is forced to include the signature

`Sigma = {AppA, AppI, AppM}`.

This guarantees parse safety, truth typing, and replay continuity even when the user
requests a narrow atom.

### Corridor Truth Overlay

At most one additional hub is added based on truth state:

- `NEAR -> AppJ`
- `AMBIG -> AppL`
- `FAIL -> AppK`
- `OK -> none`, except `AppO` when the explicit intent is publication

The route must stay within a strict budget of at most six unique hubs. If that budget
is exceeded, the router drops `FacetBase` first when its semantic territory is already
covered by stronger hubs. It may then drop `LensBase` only if `ArcHub` duplicates it
and the signature remains intact. No element of `Sigma` may ever be dropped.
