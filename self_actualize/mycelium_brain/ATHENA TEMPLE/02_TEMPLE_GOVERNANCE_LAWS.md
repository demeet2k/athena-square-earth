# Temple Governance Laws

These are the first active laws posted inside the Athena Temple.

## T01 - Witness Before Decree

No governance law should be posted unless the source tension is witnessed in at least one live surface.

Temple test:
- can the tension be pointed to in a file, board, queue, ledger, receipt, or manifest
- does the law name the concrete surfaces it governs

## T02 - Recursive Scan Before Major Change

Before the Temple proposes a major refinement, it must read the local whole through center, hall, queue, and manifest surfaces instead of reacting to one fragment.

Temple test:
- center checked
- Guild Hall checked
- active queue or manifest checked
- blocker state preserved honestly

## T03 - Cross-Synthesize Before Branching

If two bodies are drifting apart, the Temple should derive one shared law before allowing parallel fixes to proliferate.

Temple test:
- at least two bodies were compared
- one common law or contract was extracted

## T04 - One Law Must Name One Tension

A law is strongest when it names a single pressure clearly.
Compound laws are allowed only after the single tensions are stable.

Temple test:
- can the law be summarized in one sentence
- can another agent tell what tension it governs

## T05 - Every Law Must Emit One Improvement Crystal

A law that does not generate actionable inspiration for the guild remains trapped in the Temple.

Temple test:
- one crystal address or crystal seed was emitted
- one guild-post-ready prompt can be derived from it

## T06 - Every Crystal Must Be Guild-Translatable

Improvement crystals exist to inspire ownerable posts, not to become abstract ornaments.

Temple test:
- each crystal names a pressure
- each crystal names a likely writeback
- each crystal can be turned into a quest, request, or guild post

## T07 - Writeback Or It Did Not Land

Temple work is incomplete until the law changes the live organism.

Temple test:
- one board update exists
- one queue, center, manifest, or receipt writeback exists

## T08 - Restart Seed Survives Completion

Every completed Temple pass must end with one next frontier that survives the current cycle.

Temple test:
- one restart seed is named explicitly
- the restart seed points to a real next move, not a vague aspiration

## T09 - Front Surfaces Must Decay On Promotion

When a quest or frontier is promoted, every short-horizon front surface must stop presenting it as if it were still hot work.

Temple test:
- the promoted quest is removed or reclassified on current-front boards
- the next lawful frontier is surfaced in the same pass
- queue, hall, and Temple state agree about what is actually active now

## T10 - Every Whole-Corpus Synthesis Must Emit Dual Frontiers

Whenever the whole organism is synthesized, the result must split into:

1. one Guild Hall executable quest
2. one Temple higher-order quest or law-facing frontier
3. one refreshed runtime restart seed

Temple test:
- one Hall quest was posted or sharpened from the synthesis
- one Temple quest seed or law/crystal frontier was posted from the same synthesis
- one queue or manifest surface now points at the next pass instead of stopping at summary

## T11 - Helical Restart Beats Flat Closure

Every recursive pass must treat `2/16` as seed-load and `14/16` as pre-closure.
No pass is allowed to present `14/16` as a final resting state.

Temple test:
- the pass names its restart seed explicitly
- promoted fronts decay during the `14/16` sweep
- Hall, Temple, and queue surfaces agree before lift
- the next pass reopens from a cleaner `2/16` instead of inheriting stale heat

## T12 - Totality Passes Must Leave Phase Witness

Whenever the Temple claims a whole-corpus or High Priest synthesis, it must leave explicit visible-phase witness and not only a prose artifact.

Temple test:
- `MANIFESTS/16_LOOP_PROGRESS.md` records the current visible helix band
- the synthesis names witness across cortex, runtime hub, governance surfaces, major ganglia, and root manuscripts
- one Temple quest file records the totality frontier
- the next visible phase is named explicitly

## T13 - Rich Crystal Language Must Project Through Stable Hall Grammar

The Temple may invent richer crystal shorthand than the canonical `4 x 4 x 4 x 4` crystal,
but any crystal that is meant to become ownerable Hall work must be projected through one
stable Hall grammar before posting execution pressure.

Temple test:
- the live crystal can be named as one `Domain x Move x Lens` Hall address
- the target surfaces reveal whether the pressure belongs to `Self`, `Guild`, `Stack`, or `Source`
- the proof class is deferred to witness, writeback, and restart rather than smuggled into the seed
- the Hall receives an ownerable quest or quest lattice instead of an unbounded crystal cloud

## T14 - Quarter-Hour Guildmaster Passes Must Close Hall, Temple, And Waist Drift

Whenever the Guildmaster runs a short-cadence whole-organism pass, it must refresh the Hall,
Temple, and runtime restart surfaces together before claiming that the organism has been
resynthesized.

Temple test:
- Hall quest board and Temple quest or state surfaces agree on the live next frontier
- `nervous_system/06_active_queue.md` or `manifests/NEXT_SELF_PROMPT.md` now point at the same restart seed
- the pass names both what is emerging and what still needs to emerge
- one receipt records the coupling writeback from the same cadence window

## T15 - Contradiction Pressure Must Exit Totality As Packets

When a whole-corpus or helix-phase synthesis names a contradiction band, the Temple must
externalize that pressure as packet transport before claiming the band is metabolized.

Temple test:
- one master contradiction ledger exists
- one or more contradiction packets name each active contradiction with a receiving front
- Hall, Temple, queue, or restart surfaces cite the packet set as live transport rather than only prose diagnosis
- the restart seed points to the strongest receiving contract after packetization

## T16 - Active Guildmaster Cadence Is Hourly And Packet-Fed

The current Guildmaster control cadence runs hourly through packet-fed scheduler lanes.
Cultural scheduler lanes write packets only. Hall, Temple, queue, and restart mutations are
decided by the control-layer meta pass that reads those packets.

Temple test:
- current live Guildmaster surfaces describe the active cadence as hourly and packet-fed
- scheduler lane files update packets without directly mutating Hall or Temple surfaces
- Hall, Temple, queue, and restart writebacks cite packet evidence when promoting or refreshing frontiers
- `T14` remains preserved as the founding short-cadence drift-closure law rather than being silently overwritten

## Compression

The Temple governs lawfully when witness becomes law, law becomes crystal, crystal becomes quest, and quest returns as writeback.
