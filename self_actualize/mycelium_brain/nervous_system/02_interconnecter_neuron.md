# Interconnecter Neuron

## Definition

An interconnecter neuron is the minimal routing cell that binds one manuscript region to another through a shared operator, witness path, or map line.

Formal schema:

`N = (NodeID, SourceFamily, TargetFamily, Operator, WitnessSet, ReplayPath, Compression, Expansion)`

Where:

- `NodeID` is the stable neuron identifier
- `SourceFamily` and `TargetFamily` are corpus families or subfamilies
- `Operator` is the active bridge function
- `WitnessSet` is the minimal evidence required for lawful linkage
- `ReplayPath` is the deterministic re-entry route
- `Compression` is the seed form of the bridge
- `Expansion` is the full unzipped interpretation
