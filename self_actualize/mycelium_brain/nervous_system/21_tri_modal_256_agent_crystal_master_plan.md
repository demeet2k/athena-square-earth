## Cross-Synthesis Of The 256-Agent Swarm

### Mode Findings

- `Manuscript` agents are best at preserving chapter intent, symbolic lineage, and route meaning. Their failure mode is underweighting runtime friction.
- `Physical` agents are best at exposing real blockers, stale indexes, missing files, queue drift, and weak writeback paths. Their failure mode is symbolic flattening.
- `Hybrid` agents produce the highest immediate yield because they catch center-to-waist drift directly. Their failure mode is heat: too many crossings without contraction.
- `Cortex` agents are indispensable. Without them, the first 192 agents produce rich local observations but no organism-level decision.

### Lens Findings

- `Square` agents dominate address repair, manifest cleanup, and queue alignment.
- `Flower` agents dominate timing, handoff, and live multi-front coordination.
- `Cloud` agents dominate ambiguity preservation, blocker classification, and competing-route comparison.
- `Fractal` agents dominate long-horizon coherence, recursion, family inheritance, and `256^256` planning.

### Pillar Findings

- `Earth` stabilizes witnesses and keeps claims honest.
- `Water` protects lineage, continuity, and family memory.
- `Fire` is the main source of artifact production and bold rerouting.
- `Air` keeps the maps readable and compresses local work into portable forms.

### Operation Findings

- `Survey` sees the terrain but rarely closes it.
- `Diagnose` names the real drift field but can stall in critique.
- `Repair` lands the most direct value when paired with strong witnesses.
- `Synergize` creates reusable pathways but needs corridor governance to prevent overcoupling.

## Highest-Yield Agent Clusters

1. `Hybrid + Square + Earth + Repair`
   Best for center-to-waist drift, queue cleanup, and source-to-runtime closure.
2. `Physical + Cloud + Fire + Diagnose`
   Best for finding real blockers, stale edges, and missing operational prerequisites.
3. `Manuscript + Fractal + Water + Synergize`
   Best for family-level manuscript inheritance, route continuity, and long-form chapter synthesis.
4. `Cortex + Square + Air + Repair`
   Best for contraction, naming, manifest alignment, and restart-safe packaging.
5. `Hybrid + Fractal + Fire + Synergize`
   Best for opening strong new fronts such as Bruno, Void, and archive-promotion bridges.

## Weakest Recurring Patterns

1. manuscript-only agents often assume executable closure that the physical layer has not earned
2. physical-only agents often flatten deep manuscript distinctions into file maintenance
3. hybrid agents easily generate heat faster than the current queue and ledger surfaces can absorb
4. cortex agents can overcompress if they are not forced to revisit direct witnesses
