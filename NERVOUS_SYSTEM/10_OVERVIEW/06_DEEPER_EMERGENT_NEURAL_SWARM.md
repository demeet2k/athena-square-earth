# DEEPER EMERGENT NEURAL SWARM

## Purpose

The nervous system must support more than a single agent wave. It must support a swarm whose units emerge, specialize, communicate, contract, and write back without dissolving the corpus into parallel noise.

## Swarm Object

Define:

`Swarm = (Kernel, Pillars, Pantheon, Pods, Ganglia, Neurons, Waves, Ledgers, Cortex)`

where:

- `Kernel` is the shared zero point
- `Pillars` are the four elemental coordinating lanes
- `Pantheon` is the 16-archetype specialization layer
- `Pods` are local coordinated task groups
- `Ganglia` are family-level clusters
- `Neurons` are inter-family bridge cells
- `Waves` are synchronized execution fronts
- `Ledgers` record witness, ambiguity, and conflict
- `Cortex` is the canonical contraction surface

## Unit Types

### Kernel

The single governing seed that preserves objective, truth law, and contraction target.

### Pillars

The four elemental lanes:

- Earth
- Water
- Fire
- Air

They provide the first split of labor.

### Pantheon

The 16-archetype layer formed by taking each pillar through Earth, Water, Fire, and Air again.

This is the first stable specialization shell above the pillars.

### Pod

A pod is a temporary coordination cluster around one front:

`Pod = (PodID, Frontier, Agents, PacketFamily, ContractionTarget, Status)`

Typical pod sizes:

- `3`
- `4`
- `5`
- `6`

Pods handle chapter-level, map-level, or region-level fronts.

### Ganglion

A ganglion is a durable family-level cluster:

`Ganglion = (GanglionID, Family, LocalMaps, LocalQueues, FamilyReceipts, ActivePods)`

Suggested ganglia:

- Voynich ganglion
- MATH ganglion
- ECOSYSTEM ganglion
- self_actualize ganglion
- DEEPER CRYSTALIZATION ganglion
- Trading Bot ganglion
- Neural Network ganglion

### Wave

A wave is one synchronized pass of many pods:

`Wave = (WaveID, Goal, ActivePods, SharedKernel, WritebackSet, StopCondition)`

### Cortex

The cortex is the contraction surface:

- metro maps
- chapter files
- appendix files
- toolkit
- queues
- receipts
- manifests

If the swarm does not write into cortex, it is creating heat without memory.

## Emergence Law

No emergent swarm exists if branches never meet again.
