# Core Language Analysis

## Main claim

`CUT`, `AQM`, `LM`, and rotation-as-conjugacy are legitimate mathematical languages.

They are legitimate because each one has its own:

- ontology of objects,
- operator vocabulary,
- admissibility law,
- error model,
- proof or witness discipline,
- and transport rule for moving from one statement to another.

They are not identical languages. They are adjacent languages in one larger stack.

## AQM

### Primitive move

AQM replaces partial mathematics with accountable mathematics.

### Evidence

From `03_aqm_text_book.md` and `AQM MASTER TOME.docx`:

- numbers are states rather than bare scalars,
- operations are channels or instruments,
- singularities become typed boundary objects,
- approximation becomes a certified projection with ledgers,
- the base carrier is explicitly Hilbert/operator-theoretic,
- every tile exposes dependencies, output types, cert hooks, and ledger hooks.

### Why it is a language

AQM supplies:

- nouns: Q-number, density operator, channel, instrument, branch state, liminal state,
- verbs: project, transport, measure, certify, replay, escalate,
- syntax: crystal-addressed tile grammar,
- semantics: mathematical objects are state-bearing and proof-carrying,
- pragmatics: never hide approximation loss or regime shift.

### Function

AQM is the semantics engine of the stack.

It tells the system what a value is and what it means for an operation to remain accountable.

## LM

### Primitive move

LM totalizes the edge of mathematics.

### Evidence

From `LM MASTER TOME - LIMINAL MATHEMATICS (LM).docx`:

- systems are not assumed globally well-posed,
- singularity, branch ambiguity, budget exhaustion, and drift become first-class types,
- every operator must return either `BULK` or `BOUND`,
- partiality is reified as boundary rather than hidden,
- regime, corridor, and observables are formal primitives.

### Why it is a language

LM supplies:

- nouns: corridor, regime, boundary, witness, repair route, ambiguity envelope,
- verbs: totalize, detect, reclassify, guard, repair, abstain,
- syntax: typed sum of bulk and boundary outputs,
- semantics: legality is corridor-bounded and regime-relative,
- pragmatics: `ABSTAIN > GUESS` when a witness is not available.

### Function

LM is the edge calculus of the stack.

It governs what happens when a system hits singular sets, branch cuts, undefined regions, or epistemic overload.

## CUT

### Primitive move

CUT turns mathematics into an addressable crystal and a proof-carrying graph.

### Evidence

From `CUT MASTER TOME - Circle within Square within Triangle.docx`:

- the manuscript is a fixed `4^4` crystal,
- chapters and appendices are tiles with lenses, facets, and atoms,
- each statement has a local and global address,
- identity is manifest-derived,
- the mycelium graph is mandatory,
- the edge alphabet is closed and typed.

### Why it is a language

CUT supplies:

- nouns: tile, lens, facet, atom, manifest, graph, edge kind, registry epoch,
- verbs: address, route, digest, migrate, prove, conflict, instantiate,
- syntax: `ChXX<dddd>.L.F.a` style local grammar plus global manuscript identity,
- semantics: every mathematical object belongs somewhere in a navigable crystal,
- pragmatics: no untethered theorem; everything must be locatable and replay-safe.

### Function

CUT is the packaging and navigation language of the stack.

It makes total mathematics routable, indexable, and implementable.

## Rotation-as-conjugacy

### Primitive move

Rotation is not a metaphor. It is lawful transport between charts.

### Evidence

From `38_quad_holographic_rotation.md`, `Rotation is Conjugacy TOME.docx`, and `COMPLETE_MATHEMATICAL_COMPENDIUM.md`:

- representation change must preserve meaning through typed transport,
- legality is checked with explicit truth classes `OK`, `NEAR`, `AMBIG`, `FAIL`,
- forbidden moves are shadow classes rather than silent coercions,
- DFT quarter-turns, symplectic `J^2 = -I`, and opposite-face routing are treated as concrete transport laws,
- direct moves can be blocked and rerouted through legal midpoints and poles.

### Why it is a language

Rotation supplies:

- nouns: chart, conjugacy, corridor, midpoint, pole, legality class, shadow crystal,
- verbs: rotate, transport, lift, reroute, tunnel, preserve, abstain,
- syntax: typed transform between carriers and charts,
- semantics: same meaning can appear in many representations if transport obligations close,
- pragmatics: every rotation pays a cost and preserves only declared invariants.

### Function

Rotation is the translation language of the stack.

It makes multi-view mathematics lawful instead of hand-wavy.

## Distinct roles

The cleanest role split is:

- `AQM`: what a mathematical value is
- `LM`: what happens when value flow hits a boundary
- `Rotation`: how value meaning survives transport across charts
- `CUT`: how the whole thing is addressed, published, and routed

## Why the distinction matters

If these are collapsed into one language, the corpus loses precision:

- AQM loses its state semantics,
- LM loses its liminal rigor,
- rotation loses its chart-transport force,
- CUT loses its graph and address law.

The deeper insight is therefore not that they are secretly one language.

The deeper insight is that they are interoperable languages with different jobs.
