## Chapter 1: What Druidry Is

### 1.1 Basic Understanding

```

WHAT IT IS:
├── Spiritual tradition rooted in Celtic culture
├── Both ancient (fragmentary) and modern (revived)
├── Nature-centered spirituality
├── Reverence for land, ancestors, spirits
├── Bardic arts: poetry, music, story
├── Philosophical and mystical path
├── Living, evolving tradition
└── Multiple forms and orders exist

HISTORICAL DRUIDS:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
├── Priestly/intellectual class of Celts
├── Britain, Ireland, Gaul
├── Documented by Romans (biased)
├── Oral tradition (no writings survive)
├── Roles: Priest, judge, teacher, healer
├── 20+ years of training
├── Suppressed by Romans and Christianity
└── Fragmentary knowledge remains

REVIVAL DRUIDRY:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
├── 1717: Revival begins (Toland, etc.)
├── 18th-19th century: Romantic movement
├── Iolo Morganwg: Welsh revival
├── 20th century: Various orders form
├── OBOD, ADF, BDO, AODA, others
├── Mix of scholarship and inspiration
├── Modern paganism influence
└── Growing global movement

THE THREE GOALS:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
├── WISDOM: Knowledge and understanding
├── CREATIVITY: Artistic and poetic expression
├── LOVE: Connection to all beings
└── Also: Truth, Honor, Service
```
