```
└── Ritual codification

├── Imperial patronage
├── Daozang (canon) compilation
├── Complete ritual systems
├── Thunder rites developed
├── Internal alchemy emerges
└── Peak of institutional Taoism

LATER PERIOD (1200-present):
├── Quanzhen (全真) school

├── Folk religion blend
├── Modern survival

├── Northern tradition
├── Founded by Wang Chongyang (1167)

├── Internal alchemy emphasis
├── Less external ritual
├── Chan Buddhist influence
└── Major monasteries
```

```
├── Commands to spirits

├── Contracts with deities
├── Concentrated qi
└── Not mere decoration

HOW THEY WORK:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
├── Written with proper authority
├── Contains names/seals of deities
├── Activates corresponding forces
├── Commands spirits to act
├── Transforms physical material
├── Creates spiritual connections
├── Transmits priest's power
└── Works like official documents

COMPONENTS OF A TALISMAN:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. HEADER (敕令 Chìlìng):
   ├── "By Imperial Order"
   ├── Authority statement
   ├── Name of commanding deity
   └── Establishes source of power

2. BODY (符身 Fúshēn):
   ├── Main graphic content
   ├── Abbreviated characters
   ├── Combined/transformed scripts
   ├── Symbolic elements
   └── The "core" of the talisman

3. SEAL (印 Yìn):
   ├── Deity's seal/stamp
   ├── Priest's seal
   ├── Authenticates command
   └── Usually at bottom

4. ACTIVATION ELEMENTS:
   ├── Dots (circles) for power
   ├── "Hooks" for spirit binding
   ├── Directional indicators
   └── Completion marks

TYPES OF TALISMANS:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
├── PROTECTIVE (護身符): Worn for safety
├── HEALING (治病符): Cure illness
├── EXORCISM (驅邪符): Expel demons
├── SUMMONING (召請符): Call spirits
├── BLESSING (祈福符): Attract good fortune
├── BINDING (束縛符): Constrain spirits
├── PEACE (平安符): General peace
└── SPECIAL PURPOSE: Infinite variety
```

```

   ├── Fast (避穀 bìgǔ) - abstain from grains
   ├── Sexual abstinence
   ├── Bathing in infused water
   ├── Clean clothing
   ├── Meditation
   └── Mental purity essential

2. MATERIALS:
   ├── PAPER: Yellow or red (most common)
   │   └── Special talisman paper best
   ├── INK: Cinnabar (red) or black
   │   └── Mixed with water, sometimes blood
   ├── BRUSH: New, consecrated
   ├── SEAL: Priest's personal seal
   └── INCENSE: For purification

3. TIMING:
   ├── Appropriate day (based on purpose)
   ├── Planetary hours (Chinese system)
   ├── Moon phase considerations
   ├── Avoid inauspicious days
   └── Often: Dawn or midnight

THE WRITING PROCESS:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4. ALTAR SETUP:
   ├── Clean altar facing appropriate direction
   ├── Images of deities (optional)
   ├── Incense burning
   ├── Offerings present
   ├── Materials arranged
   └── Sacred space established

5. INVOCATION:
   ├── Petition appropriate deity
   ├── State purpose
   ├── Request authority to write
   ├── Recite relevant incantations
   └── Visualize deity's presence

6. WRITING (書符 Shūfú):
   ├── Breathe onto brush
   ├── Hold proper mudra with off-hand
   ├── Visualize power flowing
   ├── Write in ONE continuous action if possible
   ├── Recite incantation during writing
   ├── Each stroke has meaning
   └── Do not pause or make errors

7. ACTIVATION (開光/敕 Kāiguāng/Chì):
   ├── Recite activation formula
   ├── Breathe onto talisman
   ├── Point fingers (sword fingers) at it
   ├── Visualize it glowing with power
   ├── Stamp with seal
   └── Declare it active

8. COMPLETION:
   ├── Thank the deity
   ├── Fold properly (specific folds)
   ├── Store in clean container
   ├── Or immediately use
   └── Record the creation

SWORD FINGERS (劍指 Jiànzhǐ):
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
├── Index and middle fingers extended
├── Ring and pinky folded
├── Thumb holds folded fingers
├── Used to direct energy
├── Point at talisman to activate
└── Essential hand position
```
