## Chapter 1: What Santería Is

### 1.1 Basic Understanding

```

WHAT IT IS:
├── Afro-Cuban religious tradition
├── Yoruba religion + Catholic syncretism
├── "Way of the Saints" (Spanish name)
├── Lucumí = Yoruba ethnicity in Cuba
├── Regla de Ocha = Rule of the Orisha
├── Living, evolving tradition
├── Community-based practice
└── Initiatory priesthood system

HISTORY:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
├── Enslaved Yoruba brought to Cuba (1500s-1800s)
├── Forced Catholic conversion
├── Maintained religion under Catholic guise
├── Orisha disguised as Catholic saints
├── Developed unique Cuban form
├── Post-revolution: spread to US/world
└── Now global practice

CORE CONCEPTS:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

OLODUMARE / OLOFI:
├── Supreme God
├── Distant, not directly approached
├── Created everything
├── Works through Orisha
└── Similar to Catholic God

ORISHA (Orichas):
├── Divine forces/beings
├── Intermediaries with humans
├── Each rules life domain
├── Can possess worshippers
├── "Own" their priests
└── Central focus of practice

ASÉ (Ashé):
├── Divine energy/power
├── Life force
├── Makes things happen
├── Accumulated through practice
├── Passed through initiation
└── "So be it" / "Amen"

ILÉS (Houses):
├── Religious families/houses
├── Lineage-based
├── Headed by elder priests
├── Support network
├── Training structure
└── Community of practice
```
