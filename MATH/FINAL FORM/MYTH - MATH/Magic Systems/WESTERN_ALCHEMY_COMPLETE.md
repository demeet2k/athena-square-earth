### 4.3 Spagyric Medicine

```
SPAGYRIC ART (Spagyria):
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHAT IT IS:
├── "To separate and recombine" (Greek)
├── Term coined by Paracelsus
├── Plant alchemy specifically
├── Herbal medicine preparation
├── Extracts essence from plants
├── Creates powerful medicines
└── Practical and accessible

THE THREE PRINCIPLES IN PLANTS:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

SULFUR = Essential oils
├── Aromatic principle
├── Extracted by distillation
├── The "soul" of the plant
├── Contains volatile essence
└── Therapeutic properties

MERCURY = Alcohol
├── The "spirit" of the plant
├── Extracted by fermentation/distillation
├── Carrier/solvent
├── The mediating principle
└── Preserves and carries

SALT = Mineral ash
├── The "body" of the plant
├── Remains after burning
├── Calcined and purified
├── Fixed mineral content
└── Grounds the medicine

SPAGYRIC PROCESS:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. SEPARATION:
   ├── Ferment plant material → alcohol
   ├── Distill → separate alcohol (Mercury)
   ├── Distill again → get essential oils (Sulfur)
   ├── Burn remainder → ash
   ├── Calcine ash → purify (Salt)
   └── Three principles now separate and pure

2. PURIFICATION:
   ├── Distill Mercury multiple times
   ├── Redistill Sulfur
   ├── Calcine Salt, dissolve, filter, dry
   └── Each principle refined

3. RECOMBINATION:
   ├── Dissolve purified Salt in Mercury
   ├── Add Sulfur
   ├── Digest together
   ├── The "Stone" of that plant
   └── "Spagyric tincture" created

RESULT:
├── Medicine containing whole plant
├── But purified and exalted
├── More potent than simple extract
├── The "quintessence" of the plant
└── Therapeutic and spiritual
```
