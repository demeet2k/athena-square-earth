## Chapter 1: What Hoodoo Is

### 1.1 Basic Understanding

```

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHAT IT IS:
├── African American folk magic tradition
├── NOT a religion (no worship required)
├── Practical, results-oriented magic
├── "Rootwork" = using roots/herbs
├── "Conjure" = working spells
├── Developed during slavery era
├── Blend of African, European, Native practices
└── Living tradition, still practiced

ORIGINS:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
├── African magical traditions (Kongo, etc.)
├── European folk magic (brought by colonizers)
├── Native American herbalism
├── Biblical/Christian elements
├── Jewish mysticism (some elements)
├── Developed in American South
├── Spread through Great Migration
└── Now practiced globally

KEY DISTINCTIONS:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

HOODOO vs VOODOO:
├── Hoodoo: Folk magic PRACTICE
├── Voodoo/Vodou: RELIGION with spirits
├── Hoodoo has no required deities
├── Can be practiced by anyone
├── Often combined with Christianity
└── Different but related

THE PRACTITIONER:
├── Rootworker
├── Conjure doctor/woman
├── Two-headed doctor
├── Root doctor
├── Hoodoo man/woman
├── No formal initiation required
├── Learned from elders or self-taught
└── Skill-based, not status-based
```
