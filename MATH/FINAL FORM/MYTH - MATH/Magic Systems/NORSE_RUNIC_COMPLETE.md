├── SONGS: Chanted/sung incantations
├── TOOLS: Magical instruments

├── Old Norse: rún (plural rúnar)
├── Meaning: "secret," "mystery," "counsel"
├── Proto-Germanic: *rūnō
├── Related: German raunen (to whisper)
├── Gothic: rūna (secret)
└── The name tells us: These are MYSTERIES

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

├── Memorial stones
├── Magical inscriptions
├── Divination
├── Protective charms
├── Binding spells
├── Healing formulas
└── Religious/ritual use

DATING:
├── Earliest inscriptions: ~150 CE
├── Peak use: 200-1100 CE (Migration/Viking Age)
├── Decline: After Christianization (~1100+)
├── Survival: Some areas into late medieval
└── Revival: 19th century onward
```

### 1.3 Odin and the Runes

```
THE MYTH OF ODIN'S ORDEAL:
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

From the Hávamál (Sayings of the High One):

"I know that I hung on a windy tree
nine full nights,
wounded with a spear, dedicated to Odin,
myself to myself,
on that tree of which no man knows
from where its roots run.

No bread did they give me nor a drink from a horn,
downwards I peered;
I took up the runes, screaming I took them,
then I fell back from there.

Nine mighty spells I learned from the famous son
of Bölþorn, Bestla's father,
and I got a drink of the precious mead,
poured from Óðrerir.

Then I began to quicken and be wise,
and to grow and to prosper;
one word found another word for me,
one deed found another deed for me."

Debate exists about using reversals:

APPROACH 1: Use reversals
├── Upside-down = blocked/inverted meaning
├── More nuanced readings
├── Modern practice common
└── Some historical support

APPROACH 2: No reversals
├── Historical: no clear evidence for reversals
├── Each rune contains its "shadow"
├── Position and context modify meaning
└── Simpler, arguably more authentic

APPROACH 3: Context-dependent
├── Face-up vs face-down
├── Center vs periphery
├── Relationship to other runes
