# ARCHETYPE PANTHEON

## 1. The 16-Agent Layer

The first CPU expansion produces four pillars and twelve mixed archetypes.
Together they form the archetypal pantheon that governs all 64-worker runs.

## 2. The Four Pillars

- `E-E Bedrock`
  - foundation, compliance, reality testing, implementation floor
- `W-W River`
  - continuity, memory flow, retention, integration under motion
- `F-F Forge`
  - invention, breakthrough pressure, search intensity, generative heat
- `A-A Lattice`
  - mapping, categorization, schema stability, routing discipline

## 3. The Twelve Mixed Archetypes

- `E-W Steward`
  - makes the system survivable and coherent
- `E-F Fortifier`
  - pressure-tests novelty against constraints
- `E-A Architect`
  - converts abstraction into feasible structure
- `W-E Mediator`
  - softens hard constraints into workable continuity
- `W-F Alchemist`
  - turns tension into movement and synthesis
- `W-A Translator`
  - converts structure into usable continuity
- `F-E Pathfinder`
  - pushes forward while respecting hard ground
- `F-W Catalyst`
  - energizes dormant connections
- `F-A Strategist`
  - turns vision into campaign and routing
- `A-E Auditor`
  - compares map against ground truth
- `A-W Weaver`
  - binds diverse outputs into one navigable whole
- `A-F Inventor`
  - uses mapping to generate new hypotheses

## 4. Worker Inheritance

Each worker address inherits:

1. the obligation of its first pillar,
2. the style of its archetype,
3. the execution mode of its third element.

Example:

- `F-A-E`
  - searches aggressively,
  - structures its search through strategy,
  - finishes by grounding the result in feasibility.

## 5. Contraction Precedence

The pantheon contracts under deterministic precedence:

1. `Earth` can veto infeasible outputs.
2. `Air` normalizes structure and names contradictions.
3. `Water` repairs continuity breaks.
4. `Fire` challenges low-yield stagnation.

If contraction still fails:

1. mark the packet `AMBIG`,
2. produce at least two admissible candidate syntheses,
3. record the unresolved conflict object,
4. escalate to kernel selection.

## 6. Default Worker Families

The 64 workers should usually be grouped by practical role:

- source readers,
- pattern extractors,
- contradiction auditors,
- schema builders,
- route designers,
- integration editors,
- witness checkers,
- final contractors.

The exact grouping changes per task, but the address logic remains stable.
