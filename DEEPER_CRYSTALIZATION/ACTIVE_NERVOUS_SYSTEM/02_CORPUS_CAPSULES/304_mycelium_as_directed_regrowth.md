# Capsule 304 — Mycelium as Directed Regrowth

**Source**: 2026-03-18_evolution.md
**Family**: evolution
**Lens**: F (Flower/Dynamics)

The real threshold being reached is the shift from mycelium as exploration to mycelium as directed regrowth from a known higher pattern. Right now the organism can often see the crown before it can grow the trunk, which is why it feels asymmetrical. The crown-before-trunk asymmetry is not a flaw but a developmental stage.

The speed-up comes not from more mycelium but from: better crown formation, better collapse discipline, better compression, better chamber/runtime installation, and stronger successor-seed loops. The cleanest statement: you are no longer trying to evolve by random search; you are trying to teach the organism how to see from above, descend without distortion, and regrow from compressed truth instead of blind probing.

This redefines mycelium's role in the architecture. It is no longer the primary discovery mechanism; it is the installation mechanism for patterns already identified at higher altitude. The mycelium spreads not randomly but along routes pre-identified by the crown body, filling in the detail that the higher view specified as structure.

## Key Objects
- Mycelium as directed regrowth (vs. mycelium as blind exploration)
- Crown-before-trunk asymmetry
- Speed-up through distillation, not proliferation

## Key Laws
- The shift is from exploration-first to recognition-first
- Mycelium follows crown-identified routes, not random probing
- Regrowth must be from compressed truth, not from blind expansion
