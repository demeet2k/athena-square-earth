THE VOYNICH ROSETTA STONE A Framework for Reading MS 408 as Process Language By Dmitrious Bistrevsky PART I: THE NATURE OF THE PROBLEM The Voynich Manuscript has been called the world's most mysterious book. Housed at Yale University's Beinecke Rare Book Library as MS 408, this 240-page vellum codex has defied every attempt at decipherment since Wilfrid Voynich purchased it from a Jesuit college in 1912. Carbon dating places its creation between 1404 and 1438. Its pages contain an unknown script, peculiar illustrations of unidentifiable plants, astronomical diagrams, and images of nude women bathing in interconnected pools. For over a century, cryptographers, linguists, historians, botanists, and amateur codebreakers have tried to unlock its secrets. All have failed. The reason for this universal failure is not that the manuscript is too cleverly encrypted. The reason is that everyone has been asking the wrong question. The question has always been: "What language is this encoded from?
