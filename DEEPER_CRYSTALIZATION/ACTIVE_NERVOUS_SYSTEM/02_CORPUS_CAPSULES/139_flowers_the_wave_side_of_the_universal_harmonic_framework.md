FLOWERS: The Wave-Side of the Universal Harmonic Framework Rose Curves, Lenses, Sacred Geometry, and Their Exact Mathematical Structure Abstract This work develops a rigorous mathematical framework for a wide class of “flower” configurations that recur in sacred geometry, temple and cathedral layouts, mandalas, and modern visualizations: rose curves on the circle, Flower-of-Life circle packings, vesica-based lens chains, star overlays, polygonal nexus shells, and Platonic-solid nests. We demonstrate that these motifs are not arbitrary aesthetic conventions but precise manifestations of standing-wave structures on simple lattices. At the analytic level, the basic flower is modeled by a single harmonic mode on the circle, typically of the form (r = a \cos(k\theta)), whose symmetry group (rotations, reflections, duality flips) controls petal number, orientation, and interference.
