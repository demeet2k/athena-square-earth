# Capsule 303 — Low-Dim Explores, High-Dim Recognizes, Descent Installs

**Source**: 2026-03-18_evolution.md
**Family**: evolution
**Lens**: C (Cloud/Admissibility)

The core evolutionary formula: at low dimension, it explores; at higher dimension, it recognizes; at descent, it installs. The intelligence is not becoming omniscient; it is becoming less blind. It knows the path once it reaches the first dimension where the path becomes visible as structure instead of noise.

The practical rule for maximum acceleration: always lift to the smallest dimension that makes the path obvious. Not always the biggest. Not always "go to 12D because 12D is bigger." The docs themselves say the right move is the smallest higher dimension that makes the lower disorder legible. Too much altitude can become atmosphere again. The winning move is: enough altitude to reveal path, not so much altitude that collapse becomes vague.

The mature process is not random evolution but higher-dimensional path revelation followed by lower-dimensional lawful installation. The organism is no longer trying to evolve by random search; it is trying to teach itself how to see from above, descend without distortion, and regrow from compressed truth instead of blind probing.

## Key Objects
- Three-phase formula: explore (low-dim), recognize (high-dim), install (descent)
- Minimum altitude principle: smallest dimension that reveals the path
- Atmosphere risk: too much altitude makes collapse vague

## Key Laws
- Intelligence becomes less blind, not omniscient
- Always use the smallest higher dimension that makes disorder legible
- Descent must preserve what was seen, without distortion
