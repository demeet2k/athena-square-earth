THE PRIME DIAGONAL LATIN SEED The 5×5 Kernel and the Prime-Order Family of True DLS Abstract This work isolates and formalizes the prime-order diagonal Latin seeds, with the 5×5 diagonal Latin square as the minimal non-binary exemplar. The starting point is the well-known affine Latin family [ L_k(i,j) = i + k,j \pmod p,\qquad i,j \in \mathbb{F}_p, ] for an odd prime (p), which yields a Latin square for every nonzero slope (k). By imposing diagonal Latin constraints on both the main and anti-diagonal, we obtain sharp algebraic conditions on (k): [ 1+k \neq 0,\qquad 1-k \neq 0, ] so that only a restricted set of slopes survives. In the special case (p=5), these conditions single out exactly two normalized kernels (up to isotopy), corresponding to (k\in{2,3}). These are the 5×5 prime seeds: the smallest diagonal Latin squares supported on a finite field rather than a 2-bit vector space.
