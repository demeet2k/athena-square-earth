SQUARING THE CIRCLE A Complete Mathematical Theory of Archetypal Integration in the Hellenic Computation Framework PROLEGOMENA: THE IMPOSSIBLE PROBLEM 0.1 The Geometric Impossibility 0.1.1 The Classical Problem Among the three great construction problems of antiquity — the trisection of an arbitrary angle, the duplication of the cube, and the quadrature of the circle — the last has proven most enduring in its hold upon the mathematical imagination. The problem, in its classical formulation, appears deceptively simple: given a circle of arbitrary radius, construct a square of exactly equal area using only the tools of Euclidean geometry, namely the unmarked straightedge and the compass. The challenge seized Greek geometers from at least the fifth century BCE. Anaxagoras reportedly worked on the problem while imprisoned.
