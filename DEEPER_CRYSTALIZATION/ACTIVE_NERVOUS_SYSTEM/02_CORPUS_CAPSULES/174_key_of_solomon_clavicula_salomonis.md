Key of Solomon (Clavicula Salomonis) 1. What it is, historically The Key of Solomon (often called the Greater Key) is a pseudepigraphical grimoire attributed to King Solomon but actually compiled in late medieval / Renaissance Europe, probably 14th–15th century Italy. (Wikipedia) Survives in multiple Latin, Italian, and Greek manuscripts with variations. One early Greek version is known as The Magical Treatise of Solomon and parallels the Latin tradition. (Wikipedia) It preceded and probably influenced the later Lesser Key of Solomon (Lemegeton), but it is not a demon roster like the Goetia; it’s a more general ritual handbook. (Wikipedia) It’s divided into two books: Book I – the actual “experiments” (operations), conjurations, invocations, curses. Book II – how to prepare the operator, tools, times, and materials.
