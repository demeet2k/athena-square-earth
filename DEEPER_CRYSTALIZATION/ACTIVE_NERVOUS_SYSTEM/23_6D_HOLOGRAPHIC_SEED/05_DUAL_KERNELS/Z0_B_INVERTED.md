## Definition
Z0_B is the zero-point as seen from the **Inversion** (Face B) frame. While Z0_A represents undifferentiated potential in the forward/artifact view, Z0_B represents undifferentiated potential in the complemented view — where every element has been replaced by its opposite.

## V4 Position
- Face B operator: C(x) = 5-x / XOR (1,1)
- Z0_B = C(Z0_A): the void after inversion
- If Z0_A is "nothing yet," Z0_B is "everything already dissolved"
- Z0_A is pre-creation void; Z0_B is post-destruction void

## The Shadow Void
Z0_B is the void you arrive at by **subtracting** everything, rather than the void you start from before **adding** anything. They are algebraically identical (the group is self-inverse) but semantically opposite:
- Z0_A: emptiness as potential → creation direction
- Z0_B: emptiness as completion → dissolution direction
