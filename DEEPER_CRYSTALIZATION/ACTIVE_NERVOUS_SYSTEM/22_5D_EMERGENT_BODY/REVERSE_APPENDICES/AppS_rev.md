## The Deconstruction Protocol

AppH governs forward constructions — the step-by-step procedures that build the crystal's infrastructure. AppS_rev governs their inverse: systematic deconstruction that reveals the hidden assumptions, implicit dependencies, and unexamined foundations beneath every construction. Deconstruction is not destruction. It is the crystal's capacity for radical honesty about what it has built and why.

### The Deconstruction Method

Every forward construction in the crystal (from AppH) is a chain of steps: given inputs A and B, apply operation F to produce output C. Deconstruction reverses this chain, asking at each step:

1. **Why this operation?** — Was F the only valid operation, or were alternatives available? What criteria selected F over its competitors?
2. **Why these inputs?** — Were A and B the minimal necessary inputs, or do they carry implicit baggage that propagates through the construction?
3. **What was assumed?** — What background conditions must hold for F(A,B)=C to be valid? Are those conditions stated or hidden?
4. **What was excluded?** — What entities, operations, or pathways were implicitly excluded by choosing this construction? What would the crystal look like if a different construction had been chosen?

### The Five Deconstruction Layers

AppS_rev peels constructions apart in five layers, from surface to foundation:

| Layer | What It Reveals | Question |
|-------|----------------|----------|
| L1 — Procedural | The sequence of operations | In what order were steps taken, and does the order matter? |
| L2 — Selectional | The choices made at each step | What alternatives existed, and why were they not chosen? |
| L3 — Axiomatic | The axioms that justify the choices | What seed assumptions make these operations valid? |
| L4 — Contextual | The historical/environmental conditions | Under what circumstances was this construction designed, and do those conditions still hold? |
| L5 — Existential | The purpose the construction serves | Why does this construction exist at all? What problem does it solve, and is that problem still real? |

## Interior (4^4 skeleton)

### S-Face: The Deconstruction Calculus — disassembly as formal structure
1. **AssumptionRegister** — The formal catalogue of hidden assumptions extracted by deconstruction: each entry records the assumption, the construction it supports, the conditions under which the assumption holds, and the consequences if the assumption fails
2. **DependencyGraph** — The formal map of implicit dependencies between constructions: a directed acyclic graph where nodes are constructions and edges represent "X depends on Y's hidden assumption," revealing the true load-bearing hierarchy beneath the surface architecture
3. **AlternativeSet** — The formal collection of unchosen alternatives at each construction step: for every choice made during forward construction, the AlternativeSet records what else could have been chosen and the estimated consequences of each alternative
4. **TransparencyScore** — The formal measurement of a construction's honesty: the ratio of explicit assumptions to total assumptions (explicit + hidden), where a score of 1.0 indicates a fully transparent construction with no hidden dependencies

### F-Face: The Deconstruction Dynamics — how disassembly operates in real time
1. **LayerPeeler** — The operational process that systematically traverses the five deconstruction layers (L1-L5) for a given construction: starting at the procedural surface and drilling through selectional, axiomatic, contextual, and existential depths
2. **AssumptionExtractor** — The operational process that identifies hidden assumptions at each layer: pattern-matches construction steps against known assumption signatures and flags conditions that are relied upon but never stated
3. **AlternativeGenerator** — The operational process that generates the unchosen alternatives for each construction step: explores the possibility space of what else could have been built, providing the crystal with a map of roads not taken
4. **ReconstructionFeed** — The operational process that channels deconstruction findings back to AppH: packages revealed assumptions, dependency graphs, and alternative sets into reconstruction directives that guide the next construction cycle toward greater transparency

### C-Face: The Deconstruction Uncertainty — what remains ambiguous in disassembly itself
1. **DeconstructionCompleteness** — The uncertainty about whether all hidden assumptions have been found: deconstruction can reveal the assumptions it detects but cannot guarantee it has detected all of them, since some assumptions may be invisible to the current deconstruction method
2. **AlternativeViability** — The uncertainty about whether unchosen alternatives are truly viable: generating alternatives is easier than verifying they would have worked, and some alternatives may be theoretically possible but practically unachievable
3. **RegenerationFidelity** — The uncertainty about whether reconstruction after deconstruction produces a genuinely improved construction or merely a rearrangement with different (but equally hidden) assumptions
4. **DeconstructionParadox** — The deepest uncertainty: the deconstruction protocol is itself a construction, built on assumptions (the five-layer model, the transparency score, the dependency graph formalism) that AppS_rev must eventually deconstruct, creating an infinite regression that the crystal manages but never resolves

### R-Face: The Fractal Deconstruction — self-similar disassembly at every scale
1. **AtomDeconstruction** — Deconstruction at the smallest scale: individual entities within a cell are disassembled to reveal their implicit dependencies on neighboring entities, face membership, and positional assumptions
2. **ConstructionDeconstruction** — Deconstruction at construction scale: entire multi-step constructions from AppH are disassembled layer by layer, producing the full five-layer analysis from procedural surface to existential purpose
3. **ArchitectureDeconstruction** — Deconstruction at body scale: the emergent body's global architecture (the 9-chapter structure, the 4-face geometry, the metro topology) is disassembled to reveal the meta-assumptions that shaped the entire crystal
4. **DeconstructionDeconstruction** — The self-referential limit: AppS_rev applies its own protocol to itself, deconstructing the deconstruction method to reveal its assumptions, generating a perpetually deepening self-examination that never reaches a final foundation
