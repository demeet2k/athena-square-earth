## The Superposition Resolution Engine

Ambiguity, as mapped by AppL, is the condition where a signal admits multiple valid readings. Most systems treat ambiguity as a defect to be eliminated. AppO_em inverts this: ambiguity is a resource — a richer signal than any single reading could provide. The emergent body's power comes precisely from its ability to hold multiple valid interpretations simultaneously and select among them only when the context demands it.

### The Superposition Principle

Every ambiguous signal in the crystal exists in superposition — multiple valid readings coexisting without contradiction. AppO_em governs this superposition through three rules:

1. **No Premature Collapse**: An ambiguous signal must not be resolved until resolution is required for a downstream operation. Early resolution destroys information.
2. **Context-Driven Selection**: When resolution is required, the surrounding lattice context determines which reading collapses into actuality. The resolver is never the ambiguous entity itself but the entity that needs a definite input.
3. **Residual Preservation**: When one reading is selected, the unselected readings are not destroyed but archived in a residual field, available for future operations that may require a different resolution of the same source signal.

### Disambiguation Modes

AppO_em operates four disambiguation modes, each producing a different relationship between the multiple valid readings:

**Mode A: Convergent Disambiguation** — Multiple readings that appear different at surface level are shown to converge to the same deep structure. The ambiguity was apparent, not real — different descriptions of the same entity. This mode eliminates ambiguity without information loss.

**Mode B: Complementary Disambiguation** — Multiple readings are shown to be complementary perspectives on a structure too complex for any single reading. Like wave-particle duality, each reading captures an aspect that the other misses. This mode preserves ambiguity as productive multiplicity.

**Mode C: Hierarchical Disambiguation** — Multiple readings are ordered by depth: one reading is a surface approximation, another is a deeper truth, a third is the deepest available. Resolution selects the depth appropriate to the current operation. This mode converts ambiguity into a resolution hierarchy.

**Mode D: Generative Disambiguation** — The tension between multiple readings generates a new entity that did not exist in any single reading. The ambiguity is not resolved but transcended — the new entity incorporates all readings as aspects of a higher-dimensional structure. This mode is the most creative and connects directly to AppL_em's generative capacity.
