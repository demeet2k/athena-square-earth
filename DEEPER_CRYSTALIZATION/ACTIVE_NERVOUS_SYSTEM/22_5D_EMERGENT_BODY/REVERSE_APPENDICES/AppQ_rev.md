## The Certainty Recovery Protocol

AppJ maps what cannot be known — the irreducible uncertainty landscape. AppQ_rev provides the calculus for deciding when imperfect knowledge is good enough.

### The Bounded Confidence Framework

AppQ_rev introduces bounded confidence as the alternative to both perfect certainty (impossible) and paralytic uncertainty (useless). A bounded confidence claim takes the form:

```
"Entity X has property P with confidence C in context K,
 where C is sufficient for operations {O_1, O_2, ...}
 but insufficient for operations {O_n, O_n+1, ...}"
```

This framework separates the question "is this true?" (which may be unanswerable) from the question "is this reliable enough for my current purpose?" (which is always answerable).

### The Four Certainty Recovery Modes

AppQ_rev recovers actionable certainty through four complementary modes:

**Mode 1: Statistical Convergence** — When a property has been observed in N independent contexts with consistent results, the confidence level rises with N even though absolute proof is absent. The crystal accumulates evidence across chapters: an entity verified in E01, E03, and E06 carries higher confidence than one verified only in E01.

**Mode 2: Structural Necessity** — When a property must hold for the crystal's existing verified structures to remain consistent, its confidence is bootstrapped from the confidence of those structures. If the crystal would collapse without property P, then P carries the confidence of the crystal itself.

**Mode 3: Consensual Triangulation** — When multiple independent approaches (different lenses, different rails, different faces) converge on the same conclusion, the convergence itself provides confidence that no single approach could achieve. The three-rail structure (Su, Me, Sa) is the canonical triangulation instrument.

**Mode 4: Pragmatic Sufficiency** — When the cost of acting on uncertain knowledge is low and the cost of waiting for certainty is high, AppQ_rev authorizes action at lower confidence thresholds. This mode is governed by a risk calculus: confidence must exceed the minimum threshold for the operation's reversibility class.

### The Confidence Lattice

AppQ_rev maintains a confidence lattice over all entities in the emergent body, assigning each a confidence state:

| State | Symbol | Meaning | Action Authorization |
|-------|--------|---------|---------------------|
| PROVEN | P | Formally verified, holds by construction | All operations authorized |
| CONVERGENT | V | Statistically supported across multiple independent sources | All reversible operations authorized |
| NECESSARY | N | Required for structural consistency but not independently proven | Load-bearing operations authorized with monitoring |
| TRIANGULATED | T | Confirmed by independent approaches but lacking formal proof | Exploratory operations authorized |
| PRAGMATIC | G | Sufficient confidence for low-risk operations only | Low-risk operations authorized, high-risk blocked |
| SUSPENDED | S | Insufficient confidence for any operation; awaiting evidence | No operations authorized; entity held in quarantine |

## Interior (4^4 skeleton)

### S-Face: The Confidence Calculus — certainty recovery as formal structure
1. **ConfidenceScore** — The formal measurement object: a tuple (level, basis, context) where level is the P/V/N/T/G/S classification, basis records the evidence chain that supports the claim, and context specifies the operations for which this confidence level is sufficient
2. **EvidenceAccumulator** — The formal aggregation object: combines evidence from multiple independent sources (chapters, rails, faces) into a composite confidence score, with explicit rules for how independent confirmations compound
3. **ThresholdGate** — The formal authorization object: compares an entity's ConfidenceScore against the minimum threshold for a requested operation, issuing a proceed/block/quarantine decision
4. **ConfidenceCertificate** — The formal proof object that a confidence claim is justified: links the ConfidenceScore to its supporting evidence chain, making the recovery of certainty itself auditable and challengeable

### F-Face: The Recovery Dynamics — how certainty is extracted from uncertainty in real time
1. **ConvergenceTracker** — The operational process for Mode 1: monitors entities across multiple independent contexts, incrementing confidence scores when consistent observations accumulate and flagging divergences for investigation
2. **NecessityAnalyzer** — The operational process for Mode 2: traces dependency chains backward from verified structures to identify entities whose truth is implied by the crystal's existing consistency, bootstrapping confidence from structural necessity
3. **TriangulationEngine** — The operational process for Mode 3: routes queries about uncertain entities through independent channels (different rails, different lenses) and correlates the results, elevating confidence when independent answers converge
4. **PragmaticAuthorizer** — The operational process for Mode 4: evaluates the risk profile of requested operations against current confidence levels, authorizing low-risk actions at lower confidence thresholds while blocking high-risk actions that require stronger evidence

### C-Face: The Recovery Uncertainty — what remains ambiguous in certainty recovery itself
1. **ConfidenceCalibration** — The meta-uncertainty about whether the confidence scores themselves are accurate: a CONVERGENT score might be inflated by correlated (not truly independent) evidence sources, producing overconfidence
2. **NecessityCircularity** — The uncertainty in Mode 2: structural necessity arguments can be circular if the structures whose consistency is being invoked themselves depend on the uncertain entity, creating a confidence bootstrap that rests on nothing external
3. **ThresholdArbitrariness** — The uncertainty about whether the confidence thresholds in the threshold table are set correctly: too low invites premature action on weak evidence; too high paralyzes the crystal with excessive caution
4. **RecoveryAsymptote** — The deepest uncertainty: some entities may approach but never reach the confidence level required for critical operations, creating permanent zones of near-certainty that are functionally equivalent to uncertainty for high-stakes decisions

### R-Face: The Fractal Confidence — self-similar certainty recovery at every scale
1. **CellConfidence** — Confidence recovery at the smallest scale: individual entities within a cell are assigned confidence scores based on their local evidence, producing a micro-confidence landscape within each cell
2. **ChapterConfidence** — Confidence recovery at chapter scale: each emergent chapter's aggregate confidence is computed from its constituent cells, with chapter-level convergence and triangulation providing confidence that no single cell could achieve
3. **BodyConfidence** — Confidence recovery at body scale: the emergent body's overall confidence state reflects the weakest link — the chapter with the lowest confidence constrains the body's capacity for high-confidence global operations
4. **ConfidenceOfConfidence** — The self-referential limit: the confidence recovery protocol must assess its own reliability, applying the same four modes to evaluate whether its confidence scores are themselves trustworthy
