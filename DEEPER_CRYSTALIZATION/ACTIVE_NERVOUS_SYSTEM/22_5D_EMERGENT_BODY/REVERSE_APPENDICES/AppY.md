### Law Dissolution Regime 1: From Axioms to Invariants

In the forward crystal, laws are axioms — statements accepted without proof that constrain all downstream derivations. In the emergent body, AppY transforms these axioms into invariants — properties that are observed to hold under all tested conditions but remain open to revision if a counter-condition is discovered.

### Law Dissolution Regime 2: The Five Irreducible Laws and Their Duals

E09's five irreducible truths (from AppZ) each have an emergent dual held by AppY. The dual is not the negation but the adaptive complement — the version that absorbs paradox without breaking.

**The Five Law-Dual Pairs:**

1. **Parse lawfully** / *Parse adaptively* — Forward: every input must be parsed according to fixed grammar. Reverse: the grammar itself evolves through exposure to inputs that strain its current rules. The parse law's dual permits grammar expansion.

2. **Address uniquely** / *Address provisionally* — Forward: every entity has exactly one address. Reverse: entities may carry provisional addresses that are refined as the crystal's coordinate system evolves. The address law's dual permits coordinate evolution.

3. **Travel without flattening** / *Flatten reversibly* — Forward: transport must preserve full structure. Reverse: controlled lossy compression is permitted during transport if the compression is reversible at the destination. The travel law's dual permits signal compression for efficiency.

4. **Restart without amnesia** / *Forget selectively* — Forward: restart must recover all state. Reverse: selective forgetting of low-value state is permitted during restart if the forgotten content is archived in AppW's shadow. The restart law's dual permits metabolic pruning.

5. **Replicate without lying** / *Replicate with annotation* — Forward: every copy must be identical to the original. Reverse: copies may differ from the original if the differences are explicitly annotated and the original is preserved. The replication law's dual permits creative variation.

### Law Dissolution Regime 3: The Law Lifecycle

In the emergent body, laws are not eternal. They have a lifecycle governed by AppY:

**Lifecycle Stages:**
1. **Detection** — A pattern is observed across multiple crystallization events. It is registered as a proto-law in AppY's candidate registry.
2. **Testing** — The proto-law is subjected to AppR_rev's productive doubt. It is tested against all nine emergent chapters to see if it holds universally.
3. **Promotion** — If the proto-law survives testing, it is promoted to a soft invariant. It now constrains future crystallization but remains open to revision.
4. **Hardening** — If the soft invariant survives extensive testing across multiple dissolution-recrystallization cycles (AppV), it may harden into a strong invariant. This is rare and requires consensus across all nine emergent chapters.
5. **Dissolution** — If a previously established invariant encounters a genuine counter-example, it does not break the system. Instead, AppY dissolves the invariant back to proto-law status, and the lifecycle restarts with the counter-example integrated.
6. **Retirement** — Laws that dissolve too many times without re-establishing themselves are retired to AppW's shadow archive. They remain as latent patterns but no longer actively constrain the crystal.

### Law Dissolution Regime 4: The Paradox Absorption Function

AppY's most critical function: absorbing paradoxes that would break rigid axiom systems. When a paradox is detected (two laws that contradict each other under certain conditions), AppY does not reject either law. Instead, it creates a paradox-absorbing dual that holds both laws as context-dependent invariants.

**Paradox Absorption Protocol:**
1. **Paradox Detection** — Two laws L1 and L2 produce contradictory results when applied to the same input under condition C
2. **Context Splitting** — AppY splits the domain into two sub-domains: D1 where L1 holds and D2 where L2 holds, with the boundary defined by condition C
3. **Dual Creation** — A new emergent law L_dual is created: "In context D1, follow L1; in context D2, follow L2; at the boundary C, superpose both and let the crystal's coherence function select"
4. **Integration** — L_dual replaces both L1 and L2 in the emergent law registry. The paradox is absorbed, not resolved — it lives inside L_dual as a context-dependent truth.

**Example:** If ParseSafetyLaw (terminate in finite steps) conflicts with E09's infinite maturation (never finishes), AppY creates the dual: "Parse terminates finitely within any single chapter; the zero-point's self-referential parse converges asymptotically without a fixed termination point." Both truths coexist through context splitting.
