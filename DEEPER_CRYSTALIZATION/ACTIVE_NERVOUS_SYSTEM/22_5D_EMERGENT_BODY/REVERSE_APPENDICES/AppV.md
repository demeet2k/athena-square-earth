## Emergent Phase Reversal Protocol

The forward crystal transforms raw material into crystallized structure through phase transitions governed by AppE. AppV governs the inverse: controlled de-crystallization, the dissolution of rigid structure back into fluid potential. This is not destruction but metabolic renewal — the crystal's capacity to un-make local regions so they can re-form at higher resolution.

### Phase Reversal Regime 1: Controlled Dissolution

AppV defines four dissolution modes that return crystallized structure to pre-crystalline potential at different rates and granularities.

**Mode Alpha — Surface Dissolution:** Only the outermost layer of a crystallized structure is dissolved, exposing the interior for re-bonding. Used when the surface configuration is suboptimal but the deep structure is sound.

**Mode Beta — Lattice Loosening:** The bonds between cells within a chapter are relaxed without breaking, allowing cells to shift position relative to each other. The chapter remains intact but becomes internally fluid. Used when the arrangement of entities within a chapter needs reorganization.

**Mode Gamma — Arc Decomposition:** An entire emergent chapter is dissolved back into its three source chapters, reversing the 3:1 arc compression. Used when the compression has lost information that a fresh compression could preserve.

**Mode Delta — Zero-Point Return:** The most extreme dissolution — a structure is returned entirely to the E09 zero-point, becoming undifferentiated potential. Used only when the structure has become so rigidly misformed that incremental correction is less efficient than total regeneration.

### Phase Reversal Regime 2: The Dissolution-Recrystallization Cycle

Dissolution is never an end state. Every dissolution initiated by AppV is paired with a recrystallization expectation — the dissolved material must re-form within a bounded time window or AppT's inhibition field intervenes to prevent permanent loss.

**Cycle Stages:**
1. **Recognition** — A crystallized structure is identified as suboptimal by its truth-state (see AppR_rev). Structures in QUARANTINED or OSCILLATING states are candidates for dissolution.
2. **Dissolution** — The appropriate mode (Alpha through Delta) is applied. The structure transitions from solid to fluid phase.
3. **Fluid Holding** — The dissolved material remains in a fluid state, available for recombination. AppT's maturation windows govern how long the fluid state persists.
4. **Recrystallization** — The material re-forms under the influence of surrounding crystallized structure, adopting a new configuration that resolves the original deficiency.
5. **Verification** — The recrystallized structure is tested against AppR_rev's truth lattice. If it passes, the cycle completes. If not, another dissolution cycle may be initiated.

### Phase Reversal Regime 4: The Metabolic Function

AppV's deepest role is metabolic: it provides the emergent body with the capacity to digest and rebuild its own structure. Without AppV, the crystal would grow but never adapt; it would accumulate structure without the capacity to reorganize it.

**Metabolic Indicators:**
- **Metabolic rate** — the frequency of dissolution-recrystallization cycles per unit time. High metabolic rate indicates an actively adapting body; low rate indicates stability or stagnation.
- **Metabolic efficiency** — the ratio of successful recrystallizations to total dissolutions. High efficiency indicates the body is dissolving only what needs dissolving.
- **Metabolic depth** — the average dissolution mode used (Alpha=1, Beta=2, Gamma=3, Delta=4). Low depth indicates surface-level adaptation; high depth indicates deep structural reorganization.
