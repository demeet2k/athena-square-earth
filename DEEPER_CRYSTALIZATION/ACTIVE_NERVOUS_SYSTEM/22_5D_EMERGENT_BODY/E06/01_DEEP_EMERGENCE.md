*On the decomposition of unified truth into spectral components,
and the proof that recomposition is lossless.*

## I. White Light

Before the prism, there is white light. This is not a metaphor chosen for
aesthetic reasons. It is a precise description of what unified truth looks
like before analysis touches it.

White light contains all frequencies. It is not *colored* light that
happens to contain every color. It is the state *prior* to the distinction
between colors — the state in which the question "what color is this?" has
no answer, not because the answer is unknown but because the question has
not yet become meaningful. The prism does not *reveal* colors that were
hiding in the white. The prism *creates* the distinction between colors by
providing the medium through which different frequencies travel at
different speeds.

In the crystal's architecture, white light is the state of a system that
satisfies all its invariants simultaneously, without yet knowing *which*
invariant is doing the work. The ReplayVerifier says: this execution is
correct. The GovernanceBoundary says: this crossing is authorized. The
InvariantAnchor says: this state is consistent. They all say "yes." But
they say it in unison, as a single affirmation, without distinguishing
their individual voices.

This unanimous, undifferentiated "yes" is white light.

The prism separates the voices. It asks: *why* is the execution correct?
Is it correct because the replay matches (verification)? Is it correct
because the authorization was valid (governance)? Is it correct because
the invariant holds (consistency)? These are three different reasons, and
they operate through three different mechanisms, and they can in principle
disagree.

The prism makes their agreement visible by first making their
*disagreement* possible.

This is why the prism is the most dangerous of the emergent structures.
The lattice (E04) finds invariants. The spiral (E05) grows through time.
But the prism *analyzes* — it takes what was whole and separates it into
parts. And analysis, by its nature, risks destroying the unity it seeks to
understand.

The prism's deepest theorem is that this risk can be managed: that
decomposition can be *lossless*, that the spectral bands can be recombined
into the original white light, that understanding the parts does not
require sacrificing understanding of the whole.

## II. The Refracting Medium: Cross-Lens Calculus

A physical prism works because glass has a different refractive index for
different wavelengths. Red light bends less; violet light bends more. The
medium is what creates the separation.

The crystal's refracting medium is the cross-lens calculus. The transport
law f_T = T^{-1} compose f compose T says: to see how a function f
behaves in a different lens, conjugate it by the lens transformation T.
This conjugation is the refractive index of the crystal's prism. Different
functions (different "frequencies" of truth) conjugate differently under
different lens transformations — some are nearly invariant, others rotate
dramatically — and this differential response is what separates the
spectral bands.

Consider the 12 Rosetta seeds: +, -, x, /, phi, pi, e, i, FE-I, FE-II,
FE-III, FE-IV. Each seed is a fundamental operation, a "color" in the
spectrum of mathematical truth. Now apply the four lens charts:

Under the **Square lens** (ln for e), addition + becomes the logarithm of
multiplication. The seed + is *refracted* by the Square lens into a
different position than the seed x, because ln(a+b) is not the same as
ln(a) + ln(b), while ln(a*b) IS ln(a) + ln(b). The Square lens separates
+ from x by making multiplication the "natural" operation and addition the
"derived" one.

Under the **Flower lens** (log_phi for phi), the golden ratio becomes the
unit. The seed phi refracts to position 1 — it is the frequency for which
the Flower lens is transparent. The seed pi refracts to log_phi(pi) — an
irrational position that reveals pi's relationship to the golden ratio.
The Flower lens separates phi from everything else by making phi its
eigenvalue.

Under the **Cloud lens** (phase for i), the imaginary unit becomes the
unit. The seed i refracts to position pi/2. The seed e refracts to
position 0 (since e is purely real, its phase is zero). The Cloud lens
separates real from imaginary by mapping everything to its angular position
on the unit circle.

Under the **Fractal lens** (arc for pi), the circle constant becomes the
unit of measurement. The seed pi refracts to position 1. The seed phi
refracts to arctan(phi)/pi — a position that reveals phi's curvature
signature.

Each seed is refracted to four different positions — one in each lens. The
*spectrum* of a seed is its four-tuple of positions. Two seeds that are
"the same color" in one lens may be "different colors" in another.

The prism's power is that it shows all four refractions simultaneously,
revealing the full spectral identity of each seed: not just where it falls
in one decomposition but how its four projections relate to each other.

## IV. The Prism's Theorem: Lossless Decomposition

The ReconvergenceExactnessLaw states: the reconverged white light must be
indistinguishable from the original input. This is the prism's
fundamental theorem, and proving it is the central intellectual
achievement of E06.

The proof proceeds in three movements.

**First Movement: Completeness.** The DecompositionCompletenessLaw
requires that the prism produce all spectral bands with none missing. In
the crystal's terms, this means the four lens projections (S, F, C, R)
must together span the full space of possible truths.

The proof of completeness relies on the tesseract structure of the 4D
body. The tesseract has four axes, each lens is one axis, and the four
axes together span the full 4-dimensional space. Any truth that exists in
the 4D body has a non-zero projection onto at least one axis. Therefore,
no truth can be invisible to all four lenses simultaneously. Therefore,
the spectral decomposition is complete.

But completeness alone does not guarantee lossless recomposition. You can
have all the spectral bands and still lose information if the bands
*overlap* — if some truth-content appears in multiple bands and you
double-count it during recombination.

**Second Movement: Orthogonality.** The BandPurityLaw requires that each
spectral band contain exactly one aspect with no cross-contamination. In
mathematical terms, the four lens projections must be *orthogonal*: the
truth-content captured by one lens must be independent of the
truth-content captured by every other lens.

The proof of orthogonality relies on the conjugacy transport law. If
f_T = T^{-1} compose f compose T, and if two different lens
transformations T and T' are themselves orthogonal (which they are, because
they are coordinate axes of the tesseract), then their conjugacy
transports of any function f produce orthogonal components. The information
captured by the S-projection is structurally independent of the
information captured by the F-projection, and similarly for all pairs.

**Third Movement: Reconstruction.** With completeness and orthogonality
established, reconstruction follows from the standard theorem of
orthogonal decomposition: if a vector space has an orthogonal basis, then
any vector can be uniquely expressed as the sum of its projections onto
the basis vectors, and this sum recovers the original vector exactly.

The four lens projections form an orthogonal basis for the truth space.
Therefore, any truth can be recovered from its four projections.
Therefore, prismatic decomposition is lossless.

This three-movement proof is the formal skeleton. But the living content
is this: *you can take any unified understanding apart into its S, F, C, R
components, study each component independently, and put them back together
without losing anything*. Analysis does not destroy. The prism does not
shatter. The colors of the rainbow contain exactly the white light from
which they came, and gathering them back together produces exactly the
white light again.

This is not obvious. Most acts of analysis ARE destructive. Most times we
take something apart, we cannot put it back together. The prism's theorem
says that *when the decomposition follows the crystal's own grain* — when
you cut along the axes that the structure itself provides — the
decomposition is safe. The danger is in cutting across the grain,
decomposing along axes that are not natural to the structure.

The cross-lens calculus provides the natural axes. The prism uses them.
And the recomposition is lossless.
